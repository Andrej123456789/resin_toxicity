# Evaluation of the Genotoxicity and Cytotoxicity of Photopolymer Resin

Research project investigating toxicity of products produced by SLA 3D printer.

## Abstract (translated)

Products produced by stereolithography using photopolymer resin are becoming increasingly common, while data on their toxicity remain insufficiently studied.
The aim of this study was to determine the effect (toxicity) of these products across different stages of the process (printing, washing with isopropyl alcohol, UV curing) using the _Allium cepa_ test.
On _A. cepa_ onions exposed to products produced by stereolithography, root length, morphological changes of roots, mitotic index, proportion of cells with interphase vacuoles, and proportion of cells with chromosomal aberrations (anaphase bridges, chromosome breaks, c-mitosis, sticky chromosomes) were investigated.
The results showed that unprocessed and IPA washed resin cause inhibition of root growth. Only unprocessed resin caused morphological changes in root morphology.
Although an increased mitotic index was recorded among all resin treatments, no statistically significant deviation was observed.
The increased proportion of cells with chromosomal aberrations in the processed resin groups was not statistically significant.
The highest proportion of cells with interphase vacuoles in the IPA washed resin groups was not statistically significant.
This study confirms that unprocessed photopolymer resin shows cytotoxic and genotoxic effects, while IPA washed resin shows only cytotoxic effects, and IPA washed and UV cured resin shows only genotoxic effects.
